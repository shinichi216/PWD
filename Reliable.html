Reliable Websites and Organizations

  • National Institute of Arthritis and Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases (NIAMS): A great source for information on various musculoskeletal conditions, including arthritis, osteoporosis, and back pain.
  • American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons (AAOS): Provides patient education materials on a wide range of orthopedic topics.
  • Arthritis Foundation: Offers information and support for people with arthritis and related conditions.
  • Muscular Dystrophy Association (MDA): Provides information and support for individuals with muscular dystrophy and other neuromuscular diseases.
  • National Spinal Cord Injury Association (NSCIA): Offers resources and support for people with spinal cord injuries.

Academic Databases

  • PubMed: A free search engine accessing primarily the MEDLINE database of references and abstracts on life sciences and biomedical topics.
  • Google Scholar: A free search engine that indexes the scholarly literature across many disciplines.

Online Courses and Tutorials

  • Coursera: Offers online courses on topics like anatomy, physiology, and biomechanics.
  • edX: Provides online courses on a variety of healthcare-related topics.
  • YouTube: Many healthcare professionals and organizations post informative videos on orthopedic conditions and treatments.

Books

  • "The Human Body Book" by DK: A comprehensive visual guide to the human body.
  • "Orthopaedic Basic Science" by Simon L. Helfet: A classic textbook for medical students and orthopedic residents.
  • "Clinical Orthopaedics and Related Research": A peer-reviewed journal publishing original research articles, reviews, and case reports in orthopedics.

Remember to consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice and treatment.
